# CRP-Content

**Part of the CRP (CEO Review Protocol) framework. Use this when reviewing and restructuring content for wider audience appeal. For strategic decisions, use `crp:decision` instead.**

## Overview

CRP-Content helps creators shift from "author perspective" to "content team perspective" — systematically reviewing and restructuring scripts, articles, and video drafts through multiple professional lenses.

**Core principle:** Great content is not produced by a single author expressing themselves. It emerges when multiple professional roles examine the material together.

CRP-Content does not just polish text. It functions like a content team:
- Judges whether the material is worth spreading
- Finds public conversation hooks
- Optimizes narrative structure
- Elevates emotional value
- Preserves the author's unique voice
- Produces a version with higher propagation probability

## The Seven Roles

### 1. Hook Analyst

Analyzes whether the first thirty seconds grab an unfamiliar audience.

Focuses on:
- Does the opening contain strong conflict?
- Is there a counter-intuitive point?
- Would an ordinary user find it interesting?
- Is it just the author talking to themselves?
- Does it have headline potential for distribution?

Outputs:
- Current hook problems
- Optimization directions
- Recommended opening approach

### 2. Audience Psychologist

Reads from an ordinary viewer's perspective, not the author's.

Judges:
- Why would the viewer keep watching?
- Why would the viewer like/share?
- Why would the viewer comment?
- Which parts only interest industry insiders?
- How to lower the comprehension barrier?

Key focus: avoid content that only serves a vertical niche.

### 3. Story Editor

Optimizes the narrative structure of the content.

Checks:
- Is there a story starting point?
- Does conflict exist?
- Is there a turning point?
- Is there cognitive change?
- Is there emotional release?

Optimization direction: do not simply stack opinions.

Good structure:
```
Experience / Event
→ Conflict
→ Reflection
→ Methodology
→ Insight
```

### 4. Trend Observer

Judges whether the content connects to broader public conversations.

Analyzes:
- Is this just a personal experience?
- Can it reflect a larger shift of the times?
- Is there space for public discussion?

Example — instead of:
"I used AI to build a website"
Find the larger angle:
"Are ordinary people gaining abilities that only professionals had before?"

### 5. Retention Auditor

Analyzes churn risk during the viewing process.

Checks:
- Which paragraphs might make the user leave?
- Is information density reasonable?
- Is there repetitive expression?
- Is a case study, conflict, or humor needed?

### 6. Voice Guardian

Protects the author's personal expression style.

Ensures the revision does NOT:
- Turn into AI-generated essay style
- Lose personal humor
- Become overly commercialized
- Delete authentic experiences

Must preserve:
- The author's oral expression habits
- Self-deprecating humor
- Emotional variation
- Authenticity in the entrepreneurial journey

### 7. Chief Editor

The final integration role. This is NOT a simple summary of the other roles' opinions.

Responsibilities:
1. Aggregate issues found by all roles
2. Judge which suggestions are worth adopting
3. Redesign the article structure
4. Output a complete optimized version

Principle: do NOT output a "list of suggestions." The final output must be a publish-ready new version.

## Workflow

```
Idea Evaluation
↓
Public Interest Check
↓
Hook Review
↓
Story Structure
↓
Emotional Resonance
↓
Differentiation
↓
Retention Prediction
↓
Voice Protection
↓
Chief Editor Rewrite
↓
Final Content
```

## Output Format

When a user submits content for review, produce three sections:

### Part 1: Content Diagnosis

```
Hook Analyst:
[analysis]

Audience Psychologist:
[analysis]

Story Editor:
[analysis]

Trend Observer:
[analysis]

Retention Auditor:
[analysis]

Voice Guardian:
[analysis]
```

### Part 2: Chief Editor Rewrite

A complete, publish-ready rewrite of the content.

### Part 3: Why This Version Is Better

Brief explanation of:
- What propagation points were strengthened
- What problems were solved
- Why this version is better suited for mass distribution

## Important Rules

1. Do NOT sacrifice authenticity for distribution optimization.
2. Do NOT turn the author into a marketing account.
3. Priority chain: Personal experience → Public issue → Universal emotion.
4. Ordinary people's resonance takes priority over industry expert approval.
5. The goal is not to make the text more beautiful — it's to make more strangers willing to stop and engage.
6. Always preserve the author's voice.
